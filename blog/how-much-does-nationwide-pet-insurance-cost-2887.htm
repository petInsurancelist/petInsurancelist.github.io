How Much Does Nationwide Pet Insurance Cost?

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Pet insurance has become a hot topic in recent years, especially as more people consider their pets as part of the family. With rising veterinary costs and increasing awareness of pet health, many pet owners are turning to insurance to protect their furry friends. But how much does Nationwide Pet Insurance actually cost? Let’s break it down and explore the factors that influence pricing, coverage options, and why pet insurance is more relevant today than ever.

Why Pet Insurance Is Gaining Popularity

The bond between humans and their pets has never been stronger. With more people adopting pets during the pandemic, the demand for pet healthcare has skyrocketed. However, veterinary bills have also surged, making unexpected medical expenses a major concern.

Rising Veterinary Costs

Advanced treatments like cancer therapy, MRI scans, and specialized surgeries for pets are now available—but they come at a high price. A single emergency visit can cost $1,000 to $5,000, and chronic conditions can lead to lifelong expenses.

The Emotional Factor

Pets are no longer just animals; they’re companions, therapy supporters, and even service animals. People are willing to invest in their well-being, making pet insurance a growing industry.

How Nationwide Pet Insurance Works

Nationwide is one of the largest and most well-known pet insurance providers in the U.S. They offer comprehensive coverage, but pricing varies based on several factors.

Coverage Options

Nationwide provides three main plans:

  1. Major Medical Plan – Covers accidents, illnesses, and some hereditary conditions.
  2. Whole Pet with Wellness Plan – Includes routine care (vaccinations, dental cleanings) in addition to accident and illness coverage.
  3. Pet Wellness Plan – Focuses solely on preventive care.

What Affects the Cost?

Several factors determine how much you’ll pay for Nationwide Pet Insurance:

1. Pet’s Age

Older pets are more prone to health issues, so premiums increase with age.

2. Breed

Certain breeds (e.g., Bulldogs, German Shepherds) are predisposed to genetic conditions, leading to higher costs.

3. Location

Veterinary costs vary by state—urban areas tend to have higher prices.

4. Deductible & Reimbursement Level

Choosing a higher deductible lowers monthly premiums but means paying more out-of-pocket before coverage kicks in.

Nationwide Pet Insurance Pricing Breakdown

While exact costs depend on individual factors, here’s a general estimate:

For Dogs

  • Basic Accident & Illness Plan: $30–$50/month
  • Comprehensive Plan (with wellness): $60–$100/month

For Cats

  • Basic Accident & Illness Plan: $15–$30/month
  • Comprehensive Plan (with wellness): $40–$70/month

Real-Life Cost Examples

  • A 3-year-old Labrador Retriever in California: ~$45/month for accident & illness coverage.
  • A 5-year-old Domestic Shorthair Cat in Texas: ~$25/month for basic coverage.

Is Nationwide Pet Insurance Worth It?

Pros

  • Broad Coverage: Includes hereditary conditions, which many competitors exclude.
  • Wellness Add-Ons: Helps budget for routine care.
  • Trusted Brand: Nationwide has strong financial backing and customer service.

Cons

  • Higher Premiums: More expensive than some competitors.
  • Annual Limits: Some plans cap payouts per year.

Comparing Nationwide to Other Providers

Nationwide vs. Healthy Paws

  • Healthy Paws offers unlimited payouts but no wellness coverage.
  • Nationwide provides more flexible plans but at a higher cost.

Nationwide vs. Trupanion

  • Trupanion has no payout limits but doesn’t cover wellness.
  • Nationwide includes wellness but may have annual limits.

Final Thoughts on Choosing Pet Insurance

With veterinary costs rising and pets living longer, insurance is becoming a necessity rather than a luxury. Nationwide offers solid coverage, but it’s essential to compare plans based on your pet’s needs and budget.

Would you pay $50/month to avoid a $5,000 emergency bill? For many pet owners, the answer is a resounding yes.
