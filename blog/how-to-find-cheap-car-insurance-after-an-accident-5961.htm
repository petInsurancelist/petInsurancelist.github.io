How to Find Cheap Car Insurance After an Accident

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Getting into a car accident is stressful enough, but the aftermath—especially dealing with skyrocketing insurance premiums—can feel like salt in the wound. In today’s world, where inflation and economic uncertainty dominate headlines, finding affordable car insurance after an accident is more critical than ever. Whether you’re at fault or not, insurers often see you as a higher risk, which can lead to steep rate hikes. But don’t despair—there are proven strategies to secure cheap car insurance even after a crash.

Why Your Rates Increase After an Accident

The Risk Factor

Insurance companies base premiums on risk assessment. After an accident, you’re statistically more likely to file another claim, so insurers adjust your rates accordingly. Even if the accident wasn’t your fault, some insurers may still raise your rates simply because you were involved.

The Financial Impact

With global supply chain disruptions and rising repair costs, insurers are paying more for claims than ever before. These increased expenses are often passed down to policyholders, especially those with accidents on their records.

Steps to Find Affordable Car Insurance Post-Accident

1. Shop Around Immediately

Don’t settle for your current insurer’s renewal quote without exploring other options. Use comparison websites like NerdWallet, The Zebra, or Policygenius to get multiple quotes quickly.

Consider Smaller Insurers

Big-name companies like State Farm or Geico might not always offer the best rates after an accident. Regional insurers or lesser-known providers often have more flexible underwriting standards.

2. Ask About Accident Forgiveness

Some insurers offer accident forgiveness programs, which prevent your first at-fault accident from affecting your rates. If you weren’t at fault, make sure the accident isn’t unfairly impacting your premium.

3. Raise Your Deductible

A higher deductible means lower monthly premiums. If you can afford to pay more out of pocket in case of another accident, this strategy can significantly reduce your costs.

4. Take a Defensive Driving Course

Many insurers offer discounts for completing an approved defensive driving course. Not only will this improve your driving skills, but it could also help offset the post-accident rate hike.

5. Bundle Your Policies

Combining auto insurance with homeowners, renters, or life insurance can lead to multi-policy discounts. Companies like Progressive and Allstate often reward bundling with lower rates.

6. Improve Your Credit Score

In most U.S. states, insurers use credit-based insurance scores to determine rates. A higher credit score can help you secure better premiums, even after an accident.

7. Drive Less (If Possible)

If you can reduce your annual mileage, you may qualify for a low-mileage discount. Usage-based insurance programs (like Progressive’s Snapshot or Allstate’s Drivewise) can also reward safe driving habits.

Alternative Options If Traditional Insurers Won’t Cover You

Non-Standard Insurance Providers

If mainstream insurers deny you coverage or offer exorbitant rates, companies like The General or Dairyland specialize in high-risk drivers.

State-Assigned Risk Pools

Some states have assigned risk plans for drivers who can’t get coverage elsewhere. While these policies are often expensive, they’re better than driving uninsured.

Long-Term Strategies to Lower Rates Over Time

Maintain a Clean Driving Record

The longer you go without another accident or violation, the more your rates will decrease. Most insurers only look back 3-5 years when calculating premiums.

Re-Evaluate Your Coverage Needs

If your car is older, consider dropping collision or comprehensive coverage to save money. Just ensure you’re still meeting your state’s minimum liability requirements.

Monitor Your Rates Annually

Insurance markets fluctuate, and loyalty doesn’t always pay. Make it a habit to compare rates every year to ensure you’re still getting the best deal.

Final Thoughts

While an accident can make car insurance more expensive, it doesn’t mean you’re doomed to unaffordable rates forever. By shopping smart, leveraging discounts, and improving your risk profile, you can find cheap car insurance even after a crash. Stay proactive, and don’t let one accident keep you from securing the coverage you need at a price you can afford.
