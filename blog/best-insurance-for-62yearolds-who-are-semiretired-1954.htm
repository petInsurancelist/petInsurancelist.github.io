Best Insurance for 62-Year-Olds Who Are Semi-Retired

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Entering semi-retirement at 62 is an exciting milestone, but it also comes with unique financial and healthcare challenges. With rising medical costs, unpredictable market conditions, and evolving insurance policies, securing the right coverage is more critical than ever. Whether you're still working part-time, managing a small business, or enjoying a slower pace of life, having the best insurance can protect your health, assets, and peace of mind.

Why Insurance Matters for Semi-Retirees at 62

At 62, many Americans are in a transitional phase—no longer fully employed but not yet relying solely on Social Security or retirement savings. This makes insurance planning more complex. Here’s why:

Healthcare Costs Are Rising

Medical expenses increase with age, and without employer-sponsored health insurance, out-of-pocket costs can skyrocket. A single hospital visit or chronic condition can derail your financial stability.

Income Uncertainty

Semi-retirement often means fluctuating income. Insurance helps mitigate risks that could otherwise drain savings or force you back into full-time work.

Longevity Risks

People are living longer, which means retirement funds must stretch further. Proper insurance ensures you’re covered for long-term care, unexpected illnesses, or accidents.

Top Insurance Options for 62-Year-Old Semi-Retirees

1. Health Insurance: Medicare vs. Private Plans

At 62, you’re not yet eligible for Medicare (which starts at 65), so you’ll need alternative coverage.

COBRA

If you recently left a job, COBRA allows you to keep your employer’s health plan for up to 18 months—but it’s expensive since you pay the full premium.

Affordable Care Act (ACA) Marketplace Plans

These plans offer subsidies based on income. A Silver or Gold plan may provide better coverage for semi-retirees with moderate healthcare needs.

Short-Term Health Insurance

For those waiting for Medicare, short-term plans offer temporary coverage. However, they often exclude pre-existing conditions and have limited benefits.

2. Medicare Supplement Plans (For Those Nearing 65)

If you’re turning 65 soon, start researching Medicare options:
- Medicare Part A & B (Original Medicare) – Covers hospital and medical services.
- Medicare Advantage (Part C) – Private plans offering extra benefits like dental and vision.
- Medigap (Supplemental Insurance) – Helps cover out-of-pocket costs like copays and deductibles.

3. Long-Term Care Insurance

With nearly 70% of seniors needing long-term care at some point, this insurance is crucial. It covers nursing homes, assisted living, or in-home care—expenses Medicare doesn’t fully pay for.

4. Life Insurance: Do You Still Need It?

If you have dependents or outstanding debts (like a mortgage), term life insurance can provide financial security. However, if your kids are independent and debts are minimal, you might prioritize other coverage.

5. Disability Insurance

Even in semi-retirement, losing income due to illness or injury can be devastating. Short-term or long-term disability insurance ensures you still have cash flow if you can’t work.

6. Homeowners or Renters Insurance

Protecting your home and belongings remains essential. Review your policy to ensure adequate coverage, especially if you’ve downsized or relocated.

7. Auto Insurance Adjustments

If you’re driving less, usage-based insurance (like pay-per-mile plans) could save money without sacrificing coverage.

How to Choose the Best Insurance for Your Situation

Assess Your Needs

  • Health: Do you have chronic conditions requiring frequent care?
  • Income: Are you still earning part-time? How much can you afford in premiums?
  • Assets: Do you have significant savings to self-insure, or do you need robust coverage?

Compare Costs and Benefits

  • Look beyond premiums—consider deductibles, copays, and coverage limits.
  • Check provider networks to ensure your preferred doctors are included.

Work with an Independent Agent

Insurance brokers can help navigate complex options and find discounts you might miss on your own.

Review Policies Annually

As your semi-retirement evolves, so should your insurance. Reassess each year to avoid overpaying or being underinsured.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Assuming Medicare Starts at 62

Medicare eligibility begins at 65. If you retire earlier, you’ll need alternate health coverage.

2. Overlooking Long-Term Care Costs

Many people underestimate how expensive assisted living or in-home care can be. Planning early locks in lower premiums.

3. Dropping Life Insurance Too Soon

If you still have financial dependents or shared debts, maintaining coverage may be necessary.

4. Ignoring Inflation Protection

Some policies (like long-term care insurance) offer inflation riders to keep up with rising costs—don’t skip this feature.

Final Thoughts on Staying Protected

The right insurance mix for a 62-year-old semi-retiree balances affordability with comprehensive coverage. By evaluating your health, income, and future needs, you can build a safety net that lets you enjoy this new chapter without financial stress. Whether it’s securing health insurance before Medicare kicks in or safeguarding against long-term care expenses, proactive planning ensures you’re prepared for whatever comes next.
